# AMC Structure and Quality Review Checklist

## 1) Purpose
Use this checklist to re-review AMC report structure and product quality before additional automation, template changes, or broader rollout. The goal is consistent, evidence-based review rather than gut-feel judgment.

## 2) Review Categories

### A. Structure-First Integrity
- What to check:
  - The report frames structural tension and constraints before implications.
  - External/internal structure appears before condition or action language.
- Pass indicators:
  - Opening and diagnostic sections anchor on structural exposure, burden, and defensibility.
  - Core reading is presented before downstream condition lines.
- Fail indicators:
  - Report jumps to implied answer/action without structural setup.
  - Structural framing is shallow relative to conclusion language.

### B. Recommendation-Free Discipline
- What to check:
  - Tone remains diagnostic and conditional, not advisory.
  - No motivational or coaching-style phrasing.
- Pass indicators:
  - Language uses structural interpretation, thresholds, and conditions.
  - Reader can infer decision posture without being told what to do.
- Fail indicators:
  - Any section directly or indirectly pushes a recommendation.
  - Coaching, reassurance, or motivational phrasing appears.

### C. Comparative Quality
- What to check:
  - Comparative reports clearly differentiate option structures.
  - Comparative table/signals are interpretable and not collapsed to generic sameness.
- Pass indicators:
  - Option differences are visible in status, reading, and implication layers.
  - Comparative section feels table-first and analytically anchored.
- Fail indicators:
  - Distinct comparative cases look materially identical.
  - Comparative narrative reads as generic “option A vs B” filler.

### D. Single-Case Quality
- What to check:
  - Single-path reports carry sufficient structural depth without A/B comparison.
  - One-path viability/defensibility is concrete and non-thin.
- Pass indicators:
  - Single-case sections remain substantive and layered.
  - Decision conditions and interpretation feel case-specific.
- Fail indicators:
  - Single-case output feels materially thinner than comparative.
  - Sections become label-heavy with weak body substance.

### E. Section-Role Clarity
- What to check:
  - Each section has a distinct narrative job.
  - Adjacent sections do not echo the same sentence role/cadence.
- Pass indicators:
  - Dashboard, overview, diagnosis, interpretation, and conditions are clearly differentiated.
  - Diagnosis reads exposure-focused; conditions read threshold-focused.
- Fail indicators:
  - Sections reuse near-identical phrasing/function.
  - Reader cannot tell why a section exists separately.

### F. Content Depth
- What to check:
  - Body lines in key sections carry concrete structural information.
  - Value/mobility/temperament layers add distinct analytical value.
- Pass indicators:
  - Core sections include non-generic, case-relevant detail.
  - Section bodies are visible and not collapsed into headings only.
- Fail indicators:
  - Repeated generic lines dominate multiple sections.
  - Content appears as scaffolding rather than analysis.

### G. Visual / Premium Feel
- What to check:
  - Document feels calm, restrained, and consulting-grade.
  - Typography hierarchy and spacing support scanability.
- Pass indicators:
  - Layout reads as a premium structural memo.
  - Scored and non-scored layers are clearly separated.
- Fail indicators:
  - Template artifacts, empty scaffolding, or visual noise remain.
  - Styling feels generic, cluttered, or under-designed.

### H. Reading Flow / Coherence
- What to check:
  - Section order and block continuity support a controlled narrative arc.
  - Titles are contiguous with their own content blocks.
- Pass indicators:
  - Flow from dashboard -> analysis -> interpretation -> conditions is coherent.
  - No misordered blocks or broken transitions.
- Fail indicators:
  - Section order confusion or content appearing under wrong headings.
  - Abrupt jumps that weaken comprehension.

### I. Customer Readiness
- What to check:
  - Report is credible and usable for customer-facing delivery.
  - Language quality and section balance meet baseline production expectations.
- Pass indicators:
  - Report can be shared without major caveats.
  - Quality is consistent across representative comparative and single cases.
- Fail indicators:
  - Material weaknesses still visible in core sections.
  - Output is stable technically but not sufficiently premium/clear for customers.

## 3) Mandatory Review Questions
- Does the report show structure before answer?
- Does any section subtly push a recommendation?
- Do sections have clearly different jobs?
- Does single-case feel materially thinner than comparative?
- Are any sections still label-heavy?
- Does the document read like a premium structural memo rather than a template artifact?

## 4) Final Judgment Scale
- **Not ready**: Structural or quality gaps are too significant for pilot/customer use.
- **Internally strong but not customer-ready**: Core logic is sound but output quality is still uneven for external use.
- **Early customer-ready**: Usable for controlled customer exposure with known improvement areas.
- **Strong customer-ready**: Consistently high quality across representative cases; ready for broader customer-facing use.

## 5) Usage Note
Run this checklist before:
- pilot sample selection updates
- new template polish rounds
- new automation rollout
- future landing-page sample publication
