# AMC Landing Page Handoff (Internal)

## 1) Page Goal
This page introduces AMC as a private, structure-first career decision service and gives enough clarity for qualified users to understand fit, see representative sample cases, and choose a next step.

## 2) Section Order
1. Hero
2. What AMC Is
3. Who It Is For
4. How It Works
5. What the Report Covers
6. Sample Cases
7. Offer / Tier
8. FAQ
9. Closing CTA

## 3) Section Specs

### Hero
- Section purpose: Establish AMC positioning in one screen and route users to intake/report request.
- Final copy to use:
  - Headline: `Read the Decision Architecture Before You Commit`
  - Subheadline: `AMC is a private career architecture service for high-stakes decisions. It provides a structure-first report mapping risk, value, mobility, and commitment conditions.`
  - Primary CTA: `Start AMC Intake`
  - Secondary CTA: `Request Structural Report`
- Recommended content format: Headline + subheadline + horizontal CTA row (2 buttons).
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Keep above-the-fold concise; avoid decorative hero art; prioritize typography hierarchy and whitespace.

### What AMC Is
- Section purpose: Define AMC clearly and differentiate from coaching/generic AI advice.
- Final copy to use:
  - `AMC is a private, case-based decision brief service for professionals at meaningful crossroads. It is not generic coaching or generic AI advice; it is a structured read of decision tension, constraints, and defensibility.`
- Recommended content format: Single short paragraph.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Keep line length controlled; this is core positioning copy and should remain unchanged in meaning.

### Who It Is For
- Section purpose: Help qualified users self-identify quickly.
- Final copy to use:
  - `Experienced professionals facing consequential career decisions`
  - `People balancing compensation, stability, family constraints, and long-term positioning`
  - `Clients who want a structural decision read, not broad motivational guidance`
- Recommended content format: 3 concise bullets.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Use clean bullet rhythm; no icon-heavy treatment.

### How It Works
- Section purpose: Show process clarity and set delivery expectations.
- Final copy to use:
  1. `Submit intake context, constraints, options, and timing signals.`
  2. `Receive an AMC structural report (single-path or comparative).`
  3. `Choose delivery mode: Essential (report only) or Executive (report + 7-day chatbot access).`
- Recommended content format: Numbered 3-step row/list.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Steps should scan in under 10 seconds; keep copy unexpanded.

### What the Report Covers
- Section purpose: Translate report structure into user-readable outcomes.
- Final copy to use:
  - `Executive structural reading of the core decision tension`
  - `External pressure context (market, competition, economic, transition)`
  - `Five-factor structural scorecard and risk diagnosis`
  - `Value, mobility, and temperament interpretation layers`
  - `Decision conditions for validation, readiness, support, and commitment`
- Recommended content format: 5 bullets (or compact checklist cards).
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Keep labels customer-facing; avoid internal pipeline jargon.

### Sample Cases
- Section purpose: Provide concrete proof of case coverage and analytical style.
- Final copy to use: Use the 3 sample cards listed in Section 5 below.
- Recommended content format: 3 cards in one row (desktop), stacked (mobile).
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Keep each card text short; maintain equal card height where possible; no performance/score gimmicks.

### Offer / Tier
- Section purpose: Clarify purchase options without adding complexity.
- Final copy to use:
  - Essential: `Report only.`
  - Executive: `Report + 7-day chatbot access.`
- Recommended content format: 2 side-by-side tier blocks.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Visual hierarchy should emphasize simplicity; avoid pricing-table complexity unless required later.

### FAQ
- Section purpose: Remove common decision friction with concise answers.
- Final copy to use: Use the 6 FAQ items listed in Section 7 below.
- Recommended content format: Accordion (single-open preferred) or compact Q/A list.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Keep answers short; avoid adding extra advisory language.

### Closing CTA
- Section purpose: Provide calm, final conversion prompt.
- Final copy to use:
  - Closing line: `For high-stakes career decisions, start with a structural read before commitment.`
  - CTA 1: `Start AMC Intake`
  - CTA 2: `Request Structural Report`
- Recommended content format: Short centered CTA block + 2-button row.
- Notes for design/dev handoff: Tone should feel restrained and premium, not urgent.

## 7) FAQ Specifics (Final 6)

### 1) Is AMC career coaching?
No. AMC is a structural decision-read service.

### 2) Does AMC decide for me?
No. AMC clarifies structure, trade-offs, and conditions so you can decide with clearer constraints.

### 3) What do I receive?
An AMC report. Executive tier also includes 7-day chatbot access.

### 4) Can AMC handle comparative and single-path cases?
Yes. AMC supports both two-path comparison and one-path sustainability assessment.

### 5) Who is AMC designed for?
Professionals handling meaningful career decisions with real structural constraints.

### 6) Is AMC available for different languages?
AMC is prepared for Korean, English, and Chinese delivery workflows.

## 8) Design Guidance Notes
Use a calm, premium, structure-first visual tone. Keep typography strong and restrained. Avoid startup-style hype cues, coaching clichés, aggressive conversion language, and decorative excess. The page should read like a clear decision service brief, not a promotional campaign.
